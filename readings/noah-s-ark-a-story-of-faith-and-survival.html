Noah's Ark: A Story of Faith and Survival

Long ago, a man named Noah lived in a world that had become a very troubled place. People fought with each other, they were dishonest, and they ignored the things that truly mattered. God looked at the world and felt deep sorrow. He decided that a great flood would wash everything clean and give the world a fresh start. God chose Noah because Noah was a good and honest man. He instructed Noah to build a massive wooden boat called an ark. Noah had never built anything so large before, but he obeyed without question. He worked for many years, cutting wood, hammering planks, and sealing the walls with pitch — a thick, dark substance that kept the water out. When the ark was nearly ready, God told Noah to gather animals. Noah collected two of every creature on Earth — lions, elephants, birds, insects, and countless others. He brought enough food to sustain all of them for a very long journey. His wife, his three sons, and their wives all came aboard with him. Then the rain began. It poured for forty days and forty nights. The floodwaters rose higher and higher until they covered even the tallest mountains. Everything outside the ark perished, but Noah and his family stayed safe inside. After many months, the water slowly receded. The ark came to rest on a mountain called Ararat. Noah sent out a dove, and when it returned carrying an olive branch, he knew that dry land had appeared. Finally, Noah and his family stepped outside onto the wet earth. God placed a rainbow in the sky as a promise — the flood would never happen again. Noah looked up at the colours stretching across the sky and felt grateful for the new beginning.

❓ Comprehension Questions
  • 1. According to the text, for how many days and nights did the rain fall?
  • 2. Why did God decide to send the flood? Explain in your own words using information from the text.
  • 3. Noah had to plan carefully before the journey began. What would YOU pack or prepare if you knew you would spend many months on a boat with no way to go to a shop?
  • 4. The text says Noah "obeyed without question." Why do you think the author included this detail? What does it tell us about Noah's character compared to the other people described at the beginning?
  • 5. God used a rainbow as a symbol of his promise. Do you think a rainbow is an effective symbol for hope and a new beginning? Could another symbol work better? Explain your opinion with reasons.

✍️ Writing

Imagine you were one of Noah's children on the ark during the forty days of rain. Write a short diary entry (80–100 words) describing what you saw, heard, and felt during that time. Use at least three past tense verbs and two vocabulary words from today's lesson.
