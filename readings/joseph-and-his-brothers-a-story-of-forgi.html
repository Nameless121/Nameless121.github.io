Joseph and His Brothers: A Story of Forgiveness

Joseph was the youngest of twelve brothers. His father, Jacob, loved him very much and gave him a beautiful coat with many colors. His brothers were very jealous of Joseph. They did not like that their father loved Joseph more than them. One day, the brothers made a terrible decision. They sold Joseph to merchants who were traveling to Egypt. Jacob was very sad. He thought his son was dead. In Egypt, life was very hard for Joseph at first. He went to prison for something he did not do. But Joseph was patient and he never lost hope. He also had a special gift — he could understand dreams. One day, the king of Egypt, called Pharaoh, had a strange dream. Joseph explained the dream and warned Pharaoh that Egypt would have seven bad years with no food. Pharaoh was very grateful and made Joseph a powerful leader. Years later, Joseph's brothers came to Egypt to find food. Joseph could have been cruel to them. Instead, he chose to forgive them. His family was reunited at last.

❓ Comprehension Questions
  • 1. What gift did Jacob give to Joseph?
  • 2. Why were Joseph's brothers jealous of him? Explain in your own words.
  • 3. Joseph was patient when life was difficult. Describe a situation in your own life where you needed to be patient.
  • 4. Joseph had two chances to behave badly — when his brothers sold him, and when he met them again in Egypt. How did he respond each time? What does this tell us about his character?
  • 5. Do you think Joseph was right to forgive his brothers? Give a reason for your opinion.

✍️ Writing

Think about the moral values in Joseph's story, such as forgiveness, patience, and kindness. Write 5–7 sentences about one moral value that is important to you. Give an example from your own life or from a story you know.
