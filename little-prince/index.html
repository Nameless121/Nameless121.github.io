📖 Story Summary

Chapters 1–6: What Happened

The story begins with the narrator — a pilot — reflecting on his childhood. At age six, he drew a picture of a boa constrictor swallowing an elephant, but every grown-up he showed it to thought it was just a hat. Disappointed that adults couldn't use their imagination, he gave up drawing and became a pilot instead.

Years later, the narrator's plane breaks down in the Sahara Desert, far from any help. He is completely alone — until a strange little boy appears and asks him to draw a sheep. This is the Little Prince. The narrator is confused: how did this child get to the middle of the desert? But the Prince never answers questions directly; he only asks his own.

Through their conversations, we learn that the Little Prince comes from a tiny planet — Asteroid B-612 — barely bigger than a house. On his planet, he had three volcanoes (two active, one extinct) which he cleaned every day, and a very special rose that one day grew there on its own. The rose was beautiful but vain and demanding. Eventually, feeling confused and hurt, the Prince decided to leave and explore other worlds.

Before leaving, the Little Prince carefully cleaned his volcanoes and uprooted the baobab seedlings that threatened to destroy the planet. He said a difficult goodbye to his rose and set off on his journey through the universe.

Chapter 6

"All grown-ups were once children — although few of them remember it."

— Antoine de Saint-Exupéry

🎭 Themes

Big Ideas in Chapters 1–6

👁️
Imagination vs. Logic
Adults see a hat; the narrator sees a snake. The story celebrates seeing beyond the obvious.
🌱
Responsibility
The Prince tends his planet daily. Small, consistent care prevents big disasters.
💔
Loneliness & Longing
The sunsets reveal the Prince's sadness. Even in a universe of planets, he feels alone.
🌹
Love & Hurt
The rose is beautiful but causes pain. Love is complicated from the very start.
🔢
Numbers vs. Meaning
Grown-ups describe things in numbers; the Prince and narrator look for what truly matters.
🚀
Curiosity & Wonder
The Prince explores other worlds seeking understanding — driven by questions, not answers.

"It is only with the heart that one can see rightly; what is essential is invisible to the eye."

— Antoine de Saint-Exupéry
📖 Story Summary

Chapters 7–12: What Happened

In Chapter 7, the Little Prince asks the narrator a puzzling question: if sheep eat bushes, do they eat flowers with thorns too? He becomes very upset when the narrator dismisses the question as unimportant — because the rose on his planet has thorns, and he fears for her safety. This is our first real clue about how deeply he cares for the rose, even though she troubled him.

Chapters 8 and 9 reveal the full story of the rose. She arrived on the Prince's planet as a mysterious seed and bloomed into a proud, dramatic flower who demanded water, a glass dome for shelter, and constant attention. Though the Prince loved her, he was too inexperienced to see through her vanity and understand her love. Feeling confused and hurt, he decided to leave — saying only a formal goodbye, and instantly regretting it.

In Chapters 10 through 12, the Little Prince begins his journey through the neighbouring asteroids, each inhabited by a single adult. First he visits a King who rules over nothing but insists on commanding even the stars. Then a Conceited Man who only wants to be admired. Then a Drunkard who drinks to forget the shame of drinking — trapped in a hopeless circle. Next, a Businessman who obsessively counts the stars, believing that owning them will make him wealthy. Then a Lamplighter on a tiny planet where night and day alternate every minute — the only one the Prince feels any sympathy for, because at least he is faithful to something beyond himself. Finally, a Geographer who has never actually explored his own planet.

Each adult represents a flaw the author sees in the grown-up world: vanity, obsession, avoidance, greed, duty without meaning, and knowledge without experience.

Chapter 7
The Sheep & the Flower

The Prince asks desperately whether sheep eat flowers with thorns. His distress reveals how much he still loves his rose.

Chapter 8
The Rose Arrives

The rose grows on the Prince's planet — beautiful but vain, demanding his constant care and attention.

Chapter 9
The Farewell

The Prince leaves his planet with a cold goodbye, only to immediately regret it. He should have seen through the rose's pride to her love.

Chapter 10
The King

The Prince visits a king who commands everything — but his authority is an illusion, and he rules over no one.

Chapter 11
The Conceited Man

A man who only wants admiration. He can only hear praise — making any real conversation impossible.

Chapter 12
The Drunkard

A man who drinks to forget the shame of drinking — a sad, circular trap that the Prince finds very puzzling.

"Grown-ups love figures. When you tell them that you have made a new friend, they never ask you any questions about essential matters."

— Antoine de Saint-Exupéry

🎭 Themes

Big Ideas in Chapters 7–12

🌹
Love & Misunderstanding
The Prince loved his rose but couldn't see past her pride. Love requires patience and wisdom to understand.
👑
Power Without Purpose
The King commands everything but rules nothing. Authority that serves no one is meaningless.
🪞
Vanity & Self-Obsession
The Conceited Man only hears praise. When you only see yourself, you miss the whole universe.
🔄
Cycles of Avoidance
The Drunkard drinks to forget shame. Avoiding problems only deepens them.
💰
Ownership vs. Belonging
The Businessman thinks owning the stars makes him rich. But you cannot truly possess what you don't care for.
📚
Knowledge vs. Experience
The Geographer records but never explores. Real understanding comes from living, not just studying.

"You become responsible, forever, for what you have tamed."

— Antoine de Saint-Exupéry
